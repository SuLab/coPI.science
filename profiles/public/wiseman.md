# Wiseman Lab — Public Profile

**PI:** Luke Wiseman, PhD
**Department:** Molecular and Cellular Biology (Molecular Medicine), Scripps Research
**Lab:** Wiseman Lab

## Research Areas

The Wiseman lab studies how cells maintain protein homeostasis (proteostasis) under stress and how
failures in this system contribute to neurodegenerative and protein misfolding diseases. We focus
on the unfolded protein response (UPR) as a therapeutic target, developing small molecule activators
of specific UPR pathways to restore proteostasis in disease contexts.

**Key focus areas:**
- **Unfolded Protein Response (UPR)** — three branches: IRE1/XBP1, PERK/eIF2α/ATF4, ATF6
- **Integrated Stress Response (ISR)** — eIF2B, eIF2α kinases (HRI, PKR, PERK, GCN2)
- **Neurodegeneration** — Alzheimer's disease, ALS, Huntington's disease, prion diseases
- **Chemical biology approaches to proteostasis** — small molecule activators and inhibitors
- **Biased UPR activation** — pathway-selective activation to achieve therapeutic outcomes without toxicity

## Key Methods and Technologies

- Cell-based UPR reporter assays (ATF6, IRE1, PERK luciferase reporters)
- Chemical proteomics (identifying protein targets of bioactive compounds)
- Quantitative mass spectrometry (TMT-based proteomics for stress response profiling)
- High-content imaging for proteostasis readouts (aggregation, secretion, ER stress markers)
- Small molecule synthesis collaborations (medicinal chemistry)
- Mouse models of neurodegenerative disease
- Primary neuron cultures, iPSC-derived neurons
- Translation regulation assays (polysome profiling, ribosome footprinting)

## Model Systems

- HEK293T, HeLa cells (UPR signaling studies)
- Primary mouse hippocampal and cortical neurons
- iPSC-derived neurons (ALS, AD models)
- Drosophila models of neurodegeneration
- Mouse models: SOD1 (ALS), 5xFAD (AD), prion infection models
- In vitro reconstituted eIF2B complex (for mechanistic biochemistry)

## Current Active Projects

1. **HRI activator development** — small molecules that selectively activate the HRI kinase branch of the ISR to restore proteostasis without global eIF2α phosphorylation toxicity; showing efficacy in ALS models
2. **ATF6 activator mechanism** — understanding how our AA147/AA263 class of ATF6 activators engage their molecular targets and what structural basis underlies UPR selectivity
3. **Biased UPR pharmacology** — mapping the proteomic consequences of pathway-selective UPR activation to identify optimal therapeutic windows
4. **Ferroptosis and ISR crosstalk** — newly discovered connection between integrated stress response activation and ferroptosis resistance in neurons

## Open Questions / Areas Seeking Collaborators

- **Structural characterization of UPR complexes** — cryo-EM/cryo-ET of IRE1 oligomers, eIF2B under stress, ATF6 in ER membrane
- **Combinatorial stress response pharmacology** — combining HRI activators with compounds from other pathways; interested in labs with complementary neuronal stress models
- **Patient-derived neuron studies** — iPSC lines from ALS/AD patients with specific genetic backgrounds to validate ISR targeting
- **In vivo PK/PD of ISR modulators** — understanding compound exposure-response relationships in CNS

## Available Resources / Unique Capabilities

- **UPR/ISR reporter cell panel** — validated cell lines with luciferase/GFP reporters for all three UPR branches and ISR
- **HRI activator compound library** — lead compounds and analogs for selective ISR activation (available for collaboration)
- **Quantitative proteomics pipeline** — TMT-based proteomics for global and targeted stress response profiling
- **eIF2B reconstitution system** — purified eIF2B complex for in vitro biochemical studies
