# Ken Lab — Public Profile

**PI:** Megan Ken, PhD
**Department:** Integrative Structural and Computational Biology (ISCB), Scripps Research
**Lab:** Ken Lab

## Research Areas

The Ken lab investigates the structural biology of RNA and RNA-protein complexes, with a focus
on how RNA structure mediates viral replication and how these structures can be targeted for
antiviral drug discovery. We use NMR spectroscopy, cryo-EM, and biochemical approaches to
determine RNA structures and identify drug binding sites.

**Key focus areas:**
- **RNA structural biology** — 3D structures of functional RNA elements by NMR and cryo-EM
- **Viral RNA targeting** — identifying structured RNA elements in viral genomes (SARS-CoV-2, HIV, HCV, flaviviruses) as drug targets
- **RNA-protein interactions** — how viral RNA structures recruit host and viral proteins for replication
- **Fragment-based drug discovery against RNA** — identifying small molecule ligands for structured RNA elements
- **Antiviral lead development** — structure-guided optimization of RNA-binding compounds

## Key Methods and Technologies

- Solution NMR spectroscopy — NOESY, HSQC, relaxation experiments for RNA structure determination
- Cryo-EM of RNA-protein complexes (single-particle analysis)
- RNA chemical probing (SHAPE-MaP, DMS-MaPseq) for secondary structure mapping at transcriptome scale
- In vitro transcription and chemical synthesis of RNA constructs
- Fluorescence-based binding assays (FP, FRET) for compound screening
- Surface plasmon resonance (SPR) and ITC for binding thermodynamics
- Fragment-based screening by NMR (STD-NMR, WaterLOGSY)
- Computational RNA structure prediction (RNAfold, RNAstructure, 3D modeling)

## Model Systems

- In vitro RNA constructs (IRES elements, pseudoknots, stem-loops from SARS-CoV-2, HIV, HCV)
- Viral replicon systems for compound validation in cells
- Vero cells and primary human lung epithelial cells for antiviral activity testing
- Biochemically reconstituted RNA-protein complexes (NS5B/RNA, IRES/ribosome)

## Current Active Projects

1. **SARS-CoV-2 frameshift element** — NMR structure determination of the SARS-CoV-2 programmed -1 ribosomal frameshift site; identifying small molecules that alter frameshifting efficiency
2. **Flavivirus 3' UTR structure** — cryo-EM and NMR characterization of structured elements in ZIKA, DENV, and West Nile virus 3' UTRs that regulate translation and replication
3. **Fragment library screening against viral RNA** — systematic NMR-based fragment screening to identify starting points for RNA-targeted antiviral development
4. **RNA-targeted compound optimization** — structure-guided medicinal chemistry of fragment hits against HCV IRES and SARS-CoV-2 frameshift element

## Open Questions / Areas Seeking Collaborators

- **Cellular validation of RNA-binding compounds** — we identify compounds that bind viral RNA by biophysical methods; need labs with virological capabilities (BSL-2/BSL-3) to test antiviral activity
- **Cryo-ET of viral replication compartments** — structural visualization of viral RNA-protein complexes in infected cells; cryo-ET in situ would complement our in vitro structural data
- **Transcriptome-wide RNA structure in infected cells** — collaborators with SHAPE-MaP or icSHAPE infrastructure to map RNA structure changes during viral infection
- **Computational RNA druggability** — machine learning approaches to predict druggable RNA pockets from sequence or structure; computational collaborators to prioritize targets

## Available Resources / Unique Capabilities

- **NMR spectroscopy infrastructure** — 600 MHz and 800 MHz NMR spectrometers with cryoprobes; expertise in RNA NMR methodology
- **Viral RNA construct library** — panel of >50 characterized in vitro RNA constructs from major human RNA viruses
- **Fragment screening capability** — established NMR-based fragment screening pipeline with 500-compound library
- **RNA structure expertise** — domain expertise in viral RNA biology and RNA-protein interactions; can consult on RNA structural projects
