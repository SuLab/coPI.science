# Michael Petrascheck Lab — Public Profile

**PI:** Michael Petrascheck
**Institution:** Scripps Research Institute
**Department:** Molecular and Cellular Biology

## Research Summary

The Petrascheck lab investigates the molecular mechanisms of aging and age-related disease using an integrative approach that combines high-throughput chemical screening in C. elegans with mechanistic studies spanning invertebrate and vertebrate models. A central theme is the identification of geroprotective compounds—small molecules that extend lifespan—and the elucidation of their molecular targets. Using activity-based protein profiling and phenotypic screens, the lab has uncovered lipid signaling pathways (e.g., FAAH-4/monoacylglyceride pathway, endocannabinoid metabolism) and mitochondrial targets (ATP synthase) that regulate longevity. Recent work leverages AI-driven approaches, including graph neural networks, to design polypharmacological geroprotectors targeting multiple biogenic amine receptors with exceptional efficacy. A parallel research focus examines the interplay between energy balance, food intake, and aging, with discoveries implicating serotonergic signaling, V-ATPase, and Clic1 as regulators of feeding behavior and obesity. The lab has also established connections between mRNA translation, proteostasis, and aging, demonstrating that differential inhibition of translation initiation versus elongation modulates protein aggregation. Additionally, collaborative work has revealed how activity-dependent proteostasis, particularly through Emerin synthesis, gates neuronal plasticity. The lab develops novel computational and experimental tools, including TargetSeeker-MS for Bayesian drug-target identification and 96-well lifespan screening platforms, to accelerate geroprotector discovery and mechanistic understanding of aging biology.

## Key Methods and Technologies

- High-throughput C. elegans lifespan screening in 96-well microtiter plates
- Activity-based protein profiling (ABPP) with covalent inhibitor libraries
- Graph neural networks for polypharmacological compound prediction
- TargetSeeker-MS: Bayesian inference for drug-target discovery using energetics-based protein separation coupled to mass spectrometry
- Bacterial clearance assay for quantifying C. elegans food intake
- Metabolic labeling of nascent proteomes (BONCAT/metabolic pulse labeling)
- LC-MS/MS metabolomics (HILIC, Q Exactive hybrid quadrupole orbitrap)
- Transcriptomics and network analysis of aging gene expression
- ATP bioluminescence assay for mitochondrial function assessment
- Mouse lifespan and behavioral assays (diet-induced obesity, metabolic phenotyping)
- Pharmacological lifespan extension studies with rapamycin and novel compounds
- Electrophysiological recording of visually evoked responses in mice
