# Petrascheck Lab — Public Profile

**PI:** Michael Petrascheck, PhD
**Department:** Molecular and Cellular Biology (Molecular Medicine), Scripps Research
**Lab:** Petrascheck Lab

## Research Areas

The Petrascheck lab investigates the biology of aging and lifespan extension using C. elegans as
a primary model system. We are particularly interested in how psychoactive compounds, serotonin
signaling, and neuronal circuits regulate systemic aging — with the goal of identifying pharmacological
interventions that extend healthy lifespan.

**Key focus areas:**
- **Pharmacological lifespan extension** — high-throughput drug screening for longevity compounds in C. elegans
- **Serotonin signaling and aging** — how serotonin receptor pharmacology (especially inverse agonism) modulates lifespan
- **Neuronal regulation of aging** — how the nervous system communicates with peripheral tissues to regulate systemic aging
- **Psychoactive compounds as aging interventions** — antidepressants, antipsychotics, and their unexpected effects on lifespan
- **Integrated stress response in aging** — ISR pathways as mediators of stress-induced lifespan extension

## Key Methods and Technologies

- High-throughput C. elegans lifespan assays (automated imaging platforms)
- C. elegans drug screening (liquid culture, 96/384-well formats)
- Genetic epistasis analysis in C. elegans (RNAi knockdown, CRISPR mutants)
- Transcriptomics in C. elegans (bulk RNA-seq, aging time courses)
- Calcium imaging in C. elegans neurons (GCaMP reporters)
- Mouse lifespan and healthspan studies (metabolic phenotyping, behavioral testing)
- Mammalian cell-based assays for mechanistic follow-up (serotonin receptor signaling, ISR)
- Pharmacokinetics studies in worms and mice

## Model Systems

- C. elegans Bristol N2 (wild-type lifespan assays)
- C. elegans mutant strains (daf-2, age-1, sir-2, clk-1, and panel of lifespan mutants)
- C. elegans tissue-specific RNAi strains
- Mouse (C57BL/6, aging cohorts for metabolic and behavioral phenotyping)
- Primary mammalian cell lines (mechanistic validation of serotonin receptor pharmacology)

## Current Active Projects

1. **Atypical tetracyclines as longevity compounds** — identified a class of tetracycline derivatives that extend lifespan via ISR-independent ribosome targeting; now determining mechanism and therapeutic window
2. **Inverse agonism of serotonin receptors** — we discovered that inverse agonists at serotonin receptors (ser-7/5-HT7) extend lifespan through a neuronal circuit mechanism; defining the downstream pathway
3. **High-throughput screen expansion** — screening the FDA-approved drug library and selected compound collections for lifespan extension; identifying new longevity pharmacology
4. **Neuronal circuits of aging** — mapping how specific serotonergic and other neurons communicate to peripheral tissues to regulate aging rates

## Open Questions / Areas Seeking Collaborators

- **Combinatorial pharmacology** — testing combinations of our longevity compounds (atypical tetracyclines, serotonin receptor inverse agonists) with compounds from other labs; particularly interested in compounds affecting ISR or proteostasis
- **Molecular mechanism of ribosome-targeted longevity** — structural biology or cryo-ET of ribosomes in the presence of our atypical tetracyclines; biochemical characterization of binding mode
- **Neuroprotective effects in mammalian models** — our worm results suggest ISR-independent neuroprotection; labs with mammalian neurodegeneration models to test our compounds
- **Clinical translation of longevity pharmacology** — connections to human aging cohorts or epidemiological data to examine whether compounds in our positive screens correlate with longevity in humans

## Available Resources / Unique Capabilities

- **Automated C. elegans lifespan platform** — high-throughput worm lifespan assay running ~300 conditions simultaneously; can offer screening collaboration for compounds of interest
- **Longevity compound library** — curated collection of >200 compounds with validated lifespan-extending activity in C. elegans, with dose-response data
- **Serotonin receptor inverse agonist panel** — tool compounds for ser-7/5-HT7 and related receptors with characterized pharmacology in worms and mammalian systems
