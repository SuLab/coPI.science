# Grotjahn Lab — Public Profile

**PI:** Danielle Grotjahn, PhD
**Department:** Integrative Structural and Computational Biology (ISCB), Scripps Research
**Lab:** Grotjahn Lab

## Research Areas

The Grotjahn lab develops and applies cryo-electron tomography (cryo-ET) to visualize the
three-dimensional organization of organelles and molecular machines within intact cells at
nanometer resolution. We are particularly focused on understanding mitochondrial architecture
and dynamics, and how cellular stress signals regulate organelle morphology and function.

**Key focus areas:**
- **Cryo-electron tomography (cryo-ET)** — in situ structural biology within intact cells
- **Mitochondrial architecture** — cristae remodeling, MICOS complex, ATP synthase oligomerization, IMM organization
- **Organelle dynamics** — fission/fusion machinery (DRP1, MFN1/2), mitochondria-ER contacts, contact sites
- **Structural cell biology** — connecting molecular structure to organelle function in disease contexts
- **Surface Morphometrics Toolkit** — computational pipeline for quantitative membrane geometry analysis

## Key Methods and Technologies

- Cryo-focused ion beam milling (cryo-FIB-SEM) for cell thinning
- Cryo-ET data collection on Titan Krios and Talos Arctica (SerialEM)
- Subtomogram averaging (RELION, Dynamo)
- Surface Morphometrics Toolkit — quantitative membrane shape analysis
- Correlative light-electron microscopy (CLEM)
- Fluorescence imaging of mitochondrial dynamics (live-cell, super-resolution STED)
- Computational segmentation of cryo-ET volumes (neural network-based, manual)
- CryoDRGN for continuous heterogeneity in subtomogram averages

## Model Systems

- HeLa cells (cryo-ET of mitochondrial architecture under stress)
- MFN2-deficient MEFs (mitochondrial fusion defect model)
- Primary mouse neurons (neurodegeneration-relevant mitochondrial studies)
- Yeast Saccharomyces cerevisiae (mitochondrial membrane protein studies)
- In vitro reconstituted membrane systems (liposomes, giant unilamellar vesicles)

## Current Active Projects

1. **Cryo-ET atlas of mitochondrial architecture** — systematic mapping of cristae morphology, ATP synthase oligomerization, and MICOS complex organization across stress conditions and disease-relevant perturbations
2. **DRP1-mediated mitochondrial fission** — structural visualization of the DRP1 oligomeric spiral at the fission site; how GTP hydrolysis drives membrane constriction
3. **Mitochondria-ER contact sites** — cryo-ET characterization of MERC composition and dynamics; how contacts change during ER stress and UPR activation
4. **Surface Morphometrics for high-content structural phenotyping** — extending our computational pipeline to enable quantitative comparison of membrane geometry across experimental conditions

## Open Questions / Areas Seeking Collaborators

- **Pharmacological perturbation of mitochondrial architecture** — we can visualize structural changes; collaborators needed who can provide pharmacologically treated cells with specific mitochondrial phenotypes (UPR activators, ISR activators, mitophagy inducers)
- **Disease context** — we want to apply cryo-ET to clinically relevant questions; collaborators with patient-derived cells (neurons, cardiomyocytes) showing mitochondrial disease phenotypes
- **Membrane contact site proteomics** — structural visualization of contacts combined with proximity proteomics (BioID, TurboID) to identify unknown contact site components
- **In situ structure determination of membrane complexes** — subtomogram averaging of MICOS, respiratory chain supercomplexes in disease contexts; collaborators with specific biological questions

## Available Resources / Unique Capabilities

- **Cryo-ET infrastructure** — cryo-FIB-SEM + Titan Krios with direct detector; one of very few labs at Scripps with full cryo-FIB to cryo-ET pipeline
- **Surface Morphometrics Toolkit** — open-source pipeline for quantitative membrane geometry analysis; can be applied to tomograms from collaborators
- **Mitochondrial architecture reference dataset** — cryo-ET tomograms of mitochondria under various conditions (available for methods development)
- **Expertise in cryo-CLEM** — can correlate light microscopy phenotypes with cryo-ET structural analysis in the same cells
